# Human Finite Video / 人类有限视频

> **真实感 = 有限性留下的痕迹**
> Authenticity = Traces of Finiteness

A methodology for making AI-generated video feel like it was shot by a real person — not by replacing quality, but by injecting the constraints, hesitations, and imperfections of human perception.

## Why This Exists

AI video is getting better at generating beautiful footage. But it has a specific problem:

**It looks too perfect.**

Perfect lighting. Perfect framing. Perfect reactions. A world that perfectly serves the subject. It looks like a god is filming, not a person.

Real video feels real because a real person shot it — someone with a body, limited space, wandering attention, delayed reactions, and a camera they can't fully control.

This project turns that insight into an executable methodology.

## Core Formula

```
Authenticity = Structured High Entropy
```

AI video is too low-entropy (perfect = predictable = low information). Random noise is also high-entropy but meaningless. Human finiteness injects **causally-structured high entropy** — hesitation has psychological causes, delay has neural causes, contamination has physical causes.

## The Five Dimensions of Finiteness

| Dimension | What It Means | Human Limitation |
|-----------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Imperfection** | Logically-flawed visuals | Equipment is limited, environment is uncontrolled |
| **Latency** | Reactions aren't instant | Nervous system needs time to process |
| **Hesitation** | Judgment isn't perfect first time | Attention wanders, experience is limited |
| **Contamination** | The world doesn't serve the subject | Space is shared, environment is uncontrollable |
| **Negative Space** | Emotions aren't immediately explained | Expression is limited, silence is real |

All five point to one mother concept: **Finiteness**.

| Mode | Input | Output |
|------|-------|--------|
| **A. Generate** | Scene description / concept | Complete human-finite-video prompt |

## Key Innovation: Observer Identity

Before generating any prompt, you must answer:

```
Who is behind the camera?
- Identity: Documentary cameraman? Bystander with phone? Tired reporter?
- State: Calm? Tense? Curious? Fatigued?
- Physical position: Where are they? What limits their movement?
- Attention pattern: What are they looking for? What distracts them?
- Experience level: Veteran (controlled precision with human touch) or novice (clumsy but genuine)?
```

The observer's identity determines every camera movement, focus decision, and compositional "mistake."

## Quick Start

### The One-Line Principle

> Don't ask "How do I make it look more cinematic?"
> Ask "How do I make it look like a limited person, in a limited position, with limited reactions, captured this moment?"

### Anti-Pattern: Words That Make It Worse

Remove these from any prompt: `cinematic`, `8K`, `masterpiece`, `ultra realistic`, `perfect`, `smooth`, `pristine`, `flawless`

### Injection Snippets (paste into any prompt)

**Add hesitation:**
```
Camera slightly overshoots then gently corrects. Focus wanders briefly 
to hands/background before settling on subject's eyes. Not a search — a hesitation.
```

**Add latency:**
```
0.3-0.5s processing delay before visible response. Understanding travels 
through the body: eyes first, then face, then posture. Emotion is always a beat late.
```

**Add contamination:**
```
Background continues its own business: [element 1 continues], 
[element 2 happens without regard for camera], [foreground obstruction from space limit].
```

**Add negative space:**
```
After [key moment], hold frame for 0.7s. No cut. The emotion hasn't 
finished — it's still arriving. Don't interrupt.
```
